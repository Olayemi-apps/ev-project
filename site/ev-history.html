EV History

Electric vehicles are not new.

EVs predate mainstream petrol dominance. What changed is battery density, charging infrastructure, and software-driven power management.

Explore innovation Browse models

Timeline

Early experimentation

Early 19th-century electric propulsion concepts established the idea of battery-powered transport.

20th-century petrol dominance

Fuel availability and refuelling speed made petrol cars practical at scale.

Modern resurgence

Lithium-ion cells, power electronics, and emissions regulation accelerated EV adoption.

Performance era

Dedicated EV platforms enable repeatable performance and safer, faster charging.

Why history still matters

Understanding EV history helps buyers distinguish mature platforms from transitional solutions, especially when sourcing across markets.
